Welcome to the personal website of Gregory Perrotte, which includes information about myself and a wealth of links to both government and university weather products. Under the WxChallenge tab, you will find an array of sorted links to various weather products arranged in an easy-to-use interface for every WxChallenge location [some are still under construction] in the 2015-16 season. A partial 2016-17 schedule has been released and all current locations have a forecasting menu available. Under the Forecasting Menus tab, you will find similar resource menus for locations across Maryland and North Carolina. Please note that this site is not optimized for mobile devices. Sorry for the inconvenience!

Below you will find a comprehensive listing of my favorite weather products and data sources. All resources are split up by category and include a thumbnail of the website and a short description of the products itself. Please note that this collection of resources is compiled by myself and is not provided or endorsed by any of the content creators. All content is owned by their respective owners.

Surface Observations
Product Description Instructions Enter Station ID
National Weather Service Western Region Observations Surface observations provided by the NWS Western Region. Please note that stations outside of the western region are still valid. Enter station ID in field to right
FAA Surface Weather Observation Stations Select any state to view the ASOS and AWOS stations contained therein. You can click any of the pins followed by the identifier to view the latest observations for that station. Enter U.S. state postal abbreviation in field to right
University of Utah MesoWest Observations Detailed surface observations for the station of your choice. Enter station ID in field to right


Surface Analyses
Product Description
Weather Prediction Center Interactive Surface Analysis The WPC has an interactive surface analysis that allows you to pan and zoom around North America. Cyclones and anticyclones, fronts, SLP contours, and station plots are included.
College of DuPage Surface Analysis Surface analysis including: cyclones and anticyclones, fronts, station plots, SLP contours, 10 meter wind streamlines, and shaded dew points
(Td > 63°F).
LightningMaps.org Real Time Lightning Map This interactive map shows you lightning strikes around the world in real time. It even shows an estimated thunder wave front!
National Weather Service Western Region – Weather and Hazards Data Viewer This interactive map allows you to view watches, warnings, advisories, surface observations, radar, NWS forecasts, precipitation analyses, and fire risk information all in one place. All products displayed are customizable so you can see the data you want the way you want at the density you want.
