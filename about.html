About Me

I am a proud member of the University of North Carolina at Asheville Class of 2016. I earned a Bachelor of Science in Atmospheric Sciences and a minor in Mathematics while graduating with distinction.

I have always had a passion for weather. It all started when I was very young and received a thermometer and a rain gauge. From there it grew into drawing weather maps and as I got older, recording daily minimum and maximum temperatures as observed by my La Crosse Technology weather station.

My hobbies include hiking, fishing, gaming, weather forecasting, and now to a lesser extent golfing. During my time at UNCA, I participated in the WxChallenge and this competition has helped me to develop a love for forecasting the weather. I love the challenge of trying to predict the future and forecast verification is one of my favorite aspects of the forecast process because it helps me to fine-tune my skills, which in turn helps me to grow as a forecaster.

I have recently started the next step in my journey as an Environmental Specialist with the Forsyth County, North Carolina Government and I look forward to serving the citizens of this county for many years to come. Thank you for taking the time to read a little bit about me!
